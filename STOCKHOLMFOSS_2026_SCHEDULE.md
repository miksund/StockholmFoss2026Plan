## Keynotes — Aula Magna

### 09:00 — Opening Keynote
**"FOSS in the Age of AI Agents and Digital Sovereignty"**
*Olle Jonsson, Maintainer & Open Source Advocate*

As AI coding assistants rewrite our patches and corporations race to dominate AI infrastructure, what does software freedom mean in 2026? A reflection on maintaining human agency, Nordic values of transparency, and why community-driven development matters more than ever.

### 10:00 — Keynote
**"From Uppsala to the World: 30 Years of MySQL and the Swedish Open Source Legacy"**
*Kajsa Lindström, Database Historian & Former MySQL AB Engineer*

Sweden gave the world MySQL, Spotify's open source contributions, and a culture of pragmatic engineering. This talk traces the threads from Monty Widenius's Uppsala apartment to today's European Open Source Consortium, asking: what can we learn from Nordic FOSS history?

### 17:00 — Closing Keynote
**"Funding the Commons: New Models for Open Source Sustainability"**
*Erik Andersson, Founder of Polar*

Open source maintainers burn out while billion-dollar companies profit from their work. Polar and similar platforms are experimenting with direct funding models. But is money enough? A frank discussion about what sustainable open source actually requires.

## Main Track — Aula Magna

### 11:00
**"Supply Chain Security for the Paranoid Nordic"**
*Sigrid Holmberg, SBOM Specialist, Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket)*

How Swedish government agencies are implementing Software Bill of Materials (SBOM), package attestations, and reproducible builds. Practical lessons from securing critical public infrastructure.

### 11:30
**"Wayland on the Swedish Desktop: State of the Union"**
*Magnus Eriksson, KDE Contributor*

With KDE Plasma 6 and GNOME 47 both Wayland-first, what's the state of the Linux desktop in Sweden? Input methods for Swedish keyboards, accessibility, and why your grandfather in Norrland can finally use Linux.

### 12:00
**"The Right to Repair Meets Open Hardware"**
*Linnéa Forsberg, Repair Café Stockholm*

EU's right to repair legislation meets the open hardware movement. How Swedish maker spaces and repair cafés are using open source firmware, schematics, and 3D-printed parts to extend device lifespans.

### 14:00
**"Running Sovereign AI on Swedish Soil"**
*Dr. Henrik Nilsson, RISE Research Institutes of Sweden*

Berzelius, Sweden's AI supercomputer, runs largely on open source. But what does "sovereign AI" mean in practice? A look at running Llama, Mistral, and Swedish language models without sending data abroad.

### 14:30
**"Git's Next Decade: Lessons from the Kernel"**
*Astrid Bergman, Git Core Contributor*

Inspired by Patrick Steinhardt's FOSDEM keynote, a deep dive into Git's evolution: SHA-256 transition, scalar for massive repos, and what Swedish companies with huge monorepos (looking at you, Spotify and Klarna) actually need.

### 15:00
**"Digital Public Goods: Nordic Cooperation in Open Source"**
*Representatives from DIGG (Swedish Agency for Digital Government)*

Sweden, Norway, Denmark, and Finland are increasingly sharing open source solutions for public services. A look at Nordic cooperation projects and why open source makes cross-border digital services possible.

### 15:30
**"LibreOffice in Swedish Schools: A Progress Report"**
*Anna Lindqvist, Skolverket Digital Consultant*

Five years after several Swedish municipalities switched to LibreOffice, what have we learned? Honest assessment of successes, failures, and the eternal challenge of training teachers.

### 11:00
**"Kubernetes the Swedish Way: Lessons from Telia and Ericsson"**
*Fredrik Lund, Platform Engineer*

Large-scale K8s deployments in Swedish telecoms. Cluster federation, GitOps with Flux, and why sometimes the "boring" approach is the right one.

**"PostgreSQL Performance Tuning for Nordic Winters"**

When your database runs in a data center cooled by Swedish winter air, you optimize differently. Real-world PostgreSQL tuning stories from Swedish hosting providers.

**"NixOS in Production: A Swedish Success Story"**
*Emil Karlsson, Mullvad VPN*

How Mullvad VPN uses NixOS for reproducible, declarative infrastructure. Lessons learned from running a privacy-focused service on an immutable Linux distribution.

### 14:00
**"The Matrix Protocol: Building Federated Chat for Swedish Organizations"**
*Sofia Nordin, Element Consultant*

Swedish municipalities and universities are deploying Matrix for internal communication. Architecture decisions, bridges to legacy systems, and the road to replacing Microsoft Teams.

### 14:30
**"Observability with OpenTelemetry: From Startup to Scale"**
*Viktor Pettersson, SRE Lead*

Implementing distributed tracing and metrics collection using OpenTelemetry. How Swedish tech companies are building vendor-neutral observability stacks.

### 15:00
**"Ansible Automation in Swedish Healthcare"**
*Kristina Berg, Region Stockholm IT*

Automating infrastructure in a heavily regulated environment. Compliance, audit trails, and why healthcare IT is harder than it looks.

### 15:30
**"Running Your Own Email in 2026: Still Possible?"**
